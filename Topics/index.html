  • ECONOMIC UNION

    An economic union integrates countries with free movement of goods, services, capital, and labor, often with a single currency and unified policies.

  • LAW OF ONE PRICE

    The law of one price states that identical goods should sell for the same price in different markets, assuming no transportation costs or trade barriers.

  • PURCHASING POWER

    Purchasing power refers to the amount of goods and services that can be purchased with a given amount of money.

  • COMMON MARKET

    A common market is a custom union with common policies on product regulation, and free movement of goods, services, capital, and labor.

  • CUSTOM UNION

    A custom union is a trade agreement in which member countries establish common external tariffs and eliminate internal trade barriers.

  • PREFERENTAL AGREEMENT

    A preferential agreement provides trade benefits and reduced tariffs among participating countries.

  • MARKET INTEGRATION

    Market integration connects different markets, enabling seamless trade and stronger economic ties across regions.

  • COMPLETE ECONOMIC INTEGRATION

    It represents the highest level of regional economic cooperation, with unified policies, institutions, and often a shared currency.

  • PURCHASING POWER PARITY

    Purchasing Power Parity (PPP) compares the relative value of currencies based on the cost of goods and services in different countries.

  • FREE TRADE AREA

    A free trade area removes trade barriers like tariffs and quotas among member countries, while each retains its independent external trade policies.
